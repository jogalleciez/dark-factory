## Design Context

### Users
Hobbyist gamers who enjoy text adventures and interactive fiction. They're familiar with the genre, likely nostalgic about classic IF (Zork, Infocom), and will appreciate atmosphere over polish. They open the page on a laptop or desktop, in the evening, wanting to be drawn in.

### Brand Personality
Abandoned, corrupted, procedural. The facility has been dark a long time. Something went wrong here. The interface should feel like booting up a terminal that's been offline for years — not scary, but *unsettling in a quiet way*.

### Aesthetic Direction
Phosphor terminal. Green-on-black CRT. Authentic retro-terminal heritage, not "developer dark mode." VT323 (pixel-precise CRT font) for display text. Azeret Mono for body. OKLCH phosphor green palette. Scanlines and vignette as atmosphere. No neon, no gradients, no gloss.

Anti-references: Anything that looks like a SaaS landing page, a crypto project, or a developer portfolio. No gradient text. No glassmorphism. No hero sections with feature grids.

### Design Principles
1. **Atmosphere over decoration** — every visual choice reinforces the abandoned facility setting.
2. **Authentic, not pastiche** — use actually-correct terminal aesthetics (VT323, phosphor green, scanlines), not generic "dark coding aesthetic."
3. **Restraint in structure, precision in detail** — same single-column layout, but every spacing decision and typographic choice is deliberate.
4. **The page is the loading screen** — the user is about to step into the facility. The landing page is the moment before the door opens.
